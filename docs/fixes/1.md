## Proposed plugin lineup (mirrors the sidebar)

### 1) atmos-cli

**Scope:** "How do I use the CLI?"

- global flags, env vars, versioning
- commands (including command groups like `terraform`, `helmfile`, `packer`, `ansible`, `vendor`, `validate`, etc.)
- help/completion/docs

**Skills to include/move here**

- atmos-orchestration → `atmos-terraform`, `atmos-helmfile`, `atmos-packer`, `atmos-ansible`, `atmos-workflows`,
  `atmos-custom-commands`
- plus any "command usage" guidance now scattered elsewhere

---

### 2) atmos-cli-configuration

**Scope:** "How do I configure Atmos itself?"

- `/cli/configuration/*` (auth, components, stacks, env, settings, integrations, version, aliases, commands, describe,
  devcontainer, docs, errors, imports, logs, metadata, profiler, profiles, schemas, stores, templates, validate, vendor,
  workflows)

**Skills to include/move here**

- from atmos-configuration → `atmos-config`, `atmos-schemas`, `atmos-introspection`
- from atmos-platform → `atmos-auth`, `atmos-stores`, `atmos-toolchain`
- from atmos-integrations → `atmos-templates`, `atmos-devcontainer` (these are config-adjacent in the sidebar)

---

### 3) atmos-stacks

**Scope:** "How do I model environments and compositions?"

- `/stacks/*` (components/settings + all stacks schema concepts)

**Skills to include/move here**

- from atmos-configuration → `atmos-stacks`, `atmos-components` (if you treat components as part of stack composition)
- optionally keep "components" separate (next plugin) if you want cleaner mental boundaries

---

### 4) atmos-components

**Scope:** "How do component types work?"

- `/components/*` (Terraform/OpenTofu, Helmfile, Packer, Ansible)

**Skills to include/move here**

- either:
  - keep `atmos-components` here, and keep `atmos-stacks` purely about stack composition, **or**
  - keep `atmos-components` under atmos-stacks if your audience thinks "components are declared in stacks, therefore
    stacks plugin".

(IMO separate is cleaner, because it matches your sidebar: **Component Library** vs **Stack Configuration**.)

---

### 5) atmos-vendor

**Scope:** "How do I vendor stuff?"

- `/vendor/*` (vendor config, component manifest, url syntax)

**Skills to include/move here**

- from atmos-configuration → `atmos-vendoring`

---

### 6) atmos-validation

**Scope:** "How do I validate things?"

- `/validation/*` (json-schema, opa, editorconfig, terraform variables, "validating")

**Skills to include/move here**

- from atmos-integrations → `atmos-validation`

---

### 7) atmos-integrations

**Scope:** "How does Atmos integrate with external systems?"

- `/integrations/*` (GitHub Actions, AI Integrations, etc.)

**Skills to include/move here**

- from atmos-integrations → `atmos-gitops`
- plus any integration-specific skills you add later (GitHub Actions helpers, etc.)

---

### 8) atmos-functions

**Scope:** "How do the YAML/template functions work?"

- `/functions/yaml/*`
- `/functions/template/*`

**Skills to include/move here**

- (new) a dedicated `atmos-functions` skill group, because people will search for these directly

---

### 9) atmos-guidance

**Scope:** "How should I think about it?"

- `/design-patterns/*`
- plus opinionated "how-to" synthesis if you want it

**Skills to include/move here**

- keep your existing `atmos-design-patterns`

---

## What this fixes

- **Predictable navigation**: if someone can find it in the docs sidebar, they can find the plugin.
- **Less arbitrary**: no "platform vs orchestration" taxonomy fight.
- **Better searchability**: users look for "vendor", "stacks", "validate", "functions", not "platform".

  **
- atmos-orchestration ➜ becomes **atmos-cli**
- atmos-platform ➜ folded into **atmos-cli-configuration**
- atmos-integrations ➜ split into: **atmos-integrations**, **atmos-validation**
- atmos-guidance ➜ keep as **atmos-guidance**
